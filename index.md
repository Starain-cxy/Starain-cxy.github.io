      </p>

    <div data-lang="en" hidden>
      <p>
        I am interested in a central question: how can statistical conclusions remain trustworthy when data
        are limited and predictive models are imperfect? My current study focuses on conformal prediction,
        which provides finite-sample coverage guarantees, and prediction-powered inference, which uses machine
        learning predictions to improve the efficiency of statistical inference.
      </p>
      <p>
        I also study time series forecasting and data mining, with an interest in testing these methods on
        financial and industrial data. See <a href="/research/">Research</a> for more details.
      </p>
